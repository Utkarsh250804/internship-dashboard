Exercise 1
Understanding Functional Dependencies and Keys

1. Table Structure

EmployeeID | EmployeeName | Department | DepartmentHead | Email
-----------------------------------------------------------------
E001 | John Smith | Sales | Alice Johnson | john.smith@company.com
E002 | Mary Jones | HR | Bob Brown | mary.jones@company.com
E003 | Steve Clark | Sales | Alice Johnson | steve.clark@company.com


2. Functional Dependencies

EmployeeID -> EmployeeName
EmployeeID -> Department
EmployeeID -> Email
Department -> DepartmentHead


3. Candidate Key

The candidate key in this table is:

EmployeeID

Reason:
EmployeeID uniquely identifies each employee record in the table.


4. Attribute Dependency Explanation

EmployeeName depends on EmployeeID because each employee has a unique ID.

Department depends on EmployeeID because each employee belongs to a specific department.

Email depends on EmployeeID because each employee has a unique email.

DepartmentHead depends on Department because each department has one department head.


5. Effect on Database Design

The table has a transitive dependency:

EmployeeID -> Department
Department -> DepartmentHead

This means DepartmentHead indirectly depends on EmployeeID.

To improve database design and reduce redundancy, the table can be normalized into two tables.


Employee Table
EmployeeID | EmployeeName | Department | Email


Department Table
Department | DepartmentHead


This design improves data integrity and reduces duplication.
