Exercise 1
Data Transformation with CASE and Aggregation

1. Sample Data

ProductID | ProductName | SalesAmount
-------------------------------------
1 | Alpha | 12000
2 | Beta | 7000
3 | Gamma | 3000
4 | Delta | 4000



2. SQL Query
SELECT 
CASE 
    WHEN SalesAmount >= 10000 THEN 'High Sales'
    WHEN SalesAmount >= 5000 AND SalesAmount < 10000 THEN 'Medium Sales'
    ELSE 'Low Sales'
END AS SalesCategory,
COUNT(*) AS ProductCount
FROM Sales
GROUP BY
CASE 
    WHEN SalesAmount >= 10000 THEN 'High Sales'
    WHEN SalesAmount >= 5000 AND SalesAmount < 10000 THEN 'Medium Sales'
    ELSE 'Low Sales'
END;


3. Explanation
CASE statement is used to categorize products based on their sales amount.

High Sales : SalesAmount >= 10000
Medium Sales : SalesAmount >= 5000 and < 10000
Low Sales : SalesAmount < 5000

GROUP BY groups the products based on the sales category.

COUNT(*) counts the number of products in each category.


4. Expected Output

SalesCategory | ProductCount
-----------------------------
High Sales | 1
Medium Sales | 1
Low Sales | 2
